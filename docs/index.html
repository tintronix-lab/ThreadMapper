Frequently asked questions

The app says "No devices found"

ThreadMapper reads your Thread devices through HomeKit, so it needs two things: HomeKit access must be granted (iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → HomeKit → ThreadMapper), and you need at least one Thread border router in your home — for example a HomePod mini, Apple TV 4K, or a compatible hub. If you just want to explore the app, turn on Demo Mode in Settings.

Why is signal strength called an "estimate"?

iOS does not expose real radio RSSI for Thread accessories to third-party apps. ThreadMapper derives a response-quality estimate by timing how quickly each accessory replies. It is a reliable way to compare devices and rooms, but it is not a radio-measured dBm value — which is why the app labels it as estimated throughout.

Are the mesh links real Thread routing?

By default they are logical estimates: devices are linked to the nearest border router or same-room router. If you connect an OpenThread Border Router in Settings, ThreadMapper will use its real routing table instead.

How do I run a room survey?

Open the Survey tab and choose Guided Survey. Stand in a room, tap Start, walk around while it samples, then tap Done to save that room and move to the next. If you have an Apple Watch, you can drive Start / Done / Skip from your wrist while the survey runs on your iPhone.

Do the AI features send my data anywhere?

No. On iOS 26 and later they run entirely on device using Apple Intelligence. Nothing about your network leaves your iPhone. They require an Apple Intelligence–capable device.

I bought Pro but features are still locked

Open Settings in ThreadMapper and tap Restore Purchases. Make sure you are signed in with the same Apple Account used for the original purchase.

The Apple Watch app has no data

The watch mirrors your iPhone. Open ThreadMapper on the iPhone at least once so it can send a snapshot, and make sure the watch app is installed in the Watch app on your iPhone.
